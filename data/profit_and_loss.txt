The profit and loss statement (P&L), also called the income statement, reports a company's financial performance over a specific accounting period.

The primary purpose of the income statement is to determine whether a business generated profit or incurred a loss.

The structure typically includes:

Revenue (Sales)
Total income generated from goods or services sold.

Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs associated with producing goods or delivering services.

Gross Profit
Gross Profit = Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold

Operating Expenses
Costs incurred during normal business operations.

Examples include:
• Rent
• Salaries
• Utilities
• Marketing expenses
• Insurance

Operating Income
Operating Income = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses

Non-Operating Income and Expenses
Includes interest income, investment gains, or loan interest expenses.

Net Profit
Net Profit = Total Revenue - Total Expenses

Businesses analyze income statements to evaluate:

• profitability
• operating efficiency
• cost structure
• revenue growth trends

The income statement also plays a major role in tax calculation and financial forecasting.
