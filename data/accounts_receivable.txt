Accounts receivable represents money owed to a company by its customers for goods or services delivered on credit.

It appears as a current asset on the balance sheet.

When a sale is made on credit:

Debit: Accounts Receivable  
Credit: Sales Revenue

When the customer pays:

Debit: Cash  
Credit: Accounts Receivable

Companies monitor receivables using an aging schedule that categorizes outstanding invoices by time overdue.

Example categories:

• 0–30 days
• 31–60 days
• 61–90 days
• 90+ days

High accounts receivable may indicate strong sales but poor collection practices.
