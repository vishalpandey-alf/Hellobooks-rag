Bookkeeping is the systematic recording, organizing, and maintaining of financial transactions for a business.

Bookkeeping ensures accurate financial reporting and forms the foundation for preparing financial statements such as the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement.

Two main bookkeeping systems exist:

Single-entry bookkeeping:
Records each transaction once. Typically used by small businesses with simple financial structures.

Double-entry bookkeeping:
Records each transaction in two accounts using the accounting equation:

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

Each transaction affects at least two accounts with equal debit and credit values.

Core bookkeeping tasks include:
• Recording journal entries
• Maintaining the general ledger
• Tracking accounts receivable
• Tracking accounts payable
• Reconciling bank statements
• Maintaining payroll records
• Recording asset depreciation

Common accounting accounts include:

Assets:
Cash, accounts receivable, inventory, equipment, buildings.

Liabilities:
Accounts payable, loans payable, accrued expenses.

Equity:
Owner’s capital, retained earnings.

Accurate bookkeeping enables businesses to analyze financial performance, prepare tax filings, detect fraud, and maintain regulatory compliance.
