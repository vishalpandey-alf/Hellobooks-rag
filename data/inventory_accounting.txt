Inventory accounting tracks the cost of goods available for sale and the cost of goods sold during a financial period.

Businesses use inventory valuation methods to determine inventory cost.

Common methods include:

FIFO (First In First Out)
Assumes the oldest inventory items are sold first.

LIFO (Last In First Out)
Assumes the most recently purchased inventory is sold first.

Weighted Average Cost
Calculates average cost per unit.

Inventory valuation impacts:

• Cost of Goods Sold
• Gross Profit
• Tax liability

Lower inventory valuation increases COGS and reduces taxable income.
