Accrual accounting records revenues and expenses when they are earned or incurred, regardless of when cash is received or paid.

This method follows the matching principle, which states that expenses should be recorded in the same accounting period as the revenues they help generate.

Example:
If a company provides services in December but receives payment in January, the revenue is recorded in December.

Accrual accounting provides a more accurate picture of a company’s financial position compared to cash accounting.

Journal entry example:

When revenue is earned but cash not yet received:

Debit: Accounts Receivable  
Credit: Revenue

When cash is received later:

Debit: Cash  
Credit: Accounts Receivable
