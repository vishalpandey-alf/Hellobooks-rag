Depreciation is the systematic allocation of the cost of a tangible asset over its useful life.

Businesses depreciate assets to match the expense of using the asset with the revenue it generates.

Common depreciation methods include:

Straight-Line Method
Depreciation Expense = (Cost of Asset - Salvage Value) / Useful Life

Declining Balance Method
An accelerated depreciation method where a fixed percentage is applied to the remaining book value each year.

Double Declining Balance Method
A faster accelerated depreciation technique calculated as:

Depreciation Rate = (2 / Useful Life)

Units of Production Method
Depreciation based on actual usage of the asset.

Depreciation reduces the carrying value of assets on the balance sheet and appears as an expense on the income statement.
