An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer indicating the products or services provided and the amount owed.

Invoices are critical for revenue tracking, accounts receivable management, and taxation compliance.

A professional invoice typically contains:

• Invoice number
• Issue date
• Payment due date
• Seller information
• Buyer information
• Description of goods or services
• Quantity and unit price
• Subtotal
• Taxes (GST / VAT)
• Discounts
• Total payable amount
• Payment terms

Common payment terms include:

Net 30:
Payment is due within 30 days of the invoice date.

Net 15:
Payment due within 15 days.

Due on receipt:
Immediate payment required.

Invoices play a central role in accounts receivable management. When an invoice is issued:

Debit: Accounts Receivable  
Credit: Revenue

Once payment is received:

Debit: Cash  
Credit: Accounts Receivable

Businesses use invoicing systems to automate billing, track outstanding payments, and monitor overdue invoices.

Advanced invoice systems may integrate:

• automated payment reminders
• payment gateway integration
• invoice aging reports
• real-time revenue tracking
