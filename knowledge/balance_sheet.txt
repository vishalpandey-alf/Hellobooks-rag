A balance sheet is a financial statement that presents the financial position of a company at a specific point in time.

It follows the accounting equation:

Assets = Liabilities + Equity

The balance sheet provides insight into a company's financial stability, liquidity, and capital structure.

Assets are resources owned by the business that provide future economic benefit.

Assets are classified into:

Current assets:
Assets expected to be converted into cash within one year.

Examples:
• Cash
• Accounts receivable
• Inventory
• Short-term investments

Non-current assets:
Long-term assets used in operations.

Examples:
• Property
• Equipment
• Buildings
• Intangible assets (patents, trademarks)

Liabilities represent obligations owed to external parties.

Liabilities are classified as:

Current liabilities:
Obligations due within one year.

Examples:
• Accounts payable
• Short-term loans
• Accrued expenses
• Taxes payable

Long-term liabilities:
Obligations due after one year.

Examples:
• Bank loans
• Bonds payable
• Long-term leases

Equity represents the residual interest in the assets of the business after deducting liabilities.

Equity components include:

• Owner's capital
• Retained earnings
• Shareholder equity

Financial analysts use balance sheets to calculate important ratios such as:

Current ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities

Debt-to-equity ratio = Total Liabilities / Total Equity
